## What the paper is about

**Full title:** *From debt crises to financial crashes (and back): a stock–flow consistent model for stock price bubbles*

This is a theoretical macro-finance paper that builds a stochastic model to explain how credit and asset price bubbles form a dangerous feedback loop:

- **Core thesis:** Debt-driven economies endogenously generate boom-bust cycles — tranquility itself breeds fragility (classic Minsky). The novel angle is modeling the *reverse* direction: how credit creation causes bubbles, not just how crashes cause debt crises.

- **Methodology:** Combines Stock-Flow Consistent (SFC) macroeconomics (Keen model) with a jump-diffusion financial market model where crash intensity is *endogenous* — driven by speculative credit flows. Monte Carlo simulations in Julia.

- **Key findings:**
  - Credit expansion → asset price boom → higher crash probability (endogenous)
  - Banking sector is the critical transmission channel: rising volatility → banks raise credit spreads → investment destabilizes → recession
  - Higher base lending rates, higher volatility, and slower mean-reversion all increase crisis probability

- **Practical takeaways for investing:** Monitor speculative credit flows and lending spreads as lead indicators of systemic risk; higher volatility isn't just noise — it raises the mathematical probability of a blow-up; beware procyclical leverage.
