## Academic Research v3 — What the paper is about

"From debt crises to financial crashes (and back)" is a **stochastic macro-financial model** paper that closes a loop most models leave open. It shows how a debt-driven economy *endogenously* generates boom-bust cycles:

1. Speculative credit fuels asset price booms
2. This simultaneously raises systemic crash risk (via state-dependent jump-diffusion)
3. When the crash hits, banks *raise* lending rates (procyclical), which spikes firms' debt burden
4. That triggers self-reinforcing output collapse → feeding back into more debt distress

**Core novelty:** Couples a Stock-Flow Consistent (Keen-style) macro model with a jump-diffusion financial market block where crash probability is **endogenous to speculative credit flows** — not a fixed parameter. The result aligns with Minsky's Financial Instability Hypothesis and shows that mean reversion speed and baseline interest rates are key levers for crisis frequency.
