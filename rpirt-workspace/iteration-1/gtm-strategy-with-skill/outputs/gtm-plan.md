# Go-to-Market Strategy: Legal Intake Automation SaaS
### Small Law Firms | Months 1–6 Post-Launch

**Prepared:** April 2026  
**Budget:** $15,000  
**Starting point:** 3 pilot customers, 0 paying  
**Target:** 20 paying customers by end of Month 6  
**Projected MRR at target:** ~$2,980/month (at $149 avg. MRR)  
**Projected ARR at target:** ~$35,760

---

## Executive Summary

You have a product that solves a real, measurable problem — law firms that use manual intake
processes lose an estimated 33% of potential clients to slow response times. Your three pilot
customers prove the product works. The challenge is converting pilots and building a repeatable
customer acquisition engine on a $15,000 budget.

The strategy below is deliberately unscalable in the right way. For months 1–4, the founder runs
every sale personally — high-touch, high-trust, sales-led. This is correct for legal buyers and
for the 0→20 customer range. Scale comes after proof, not before.

**The one-sentence GTM strategy:** Convert pilots with urgency, build case studies, then run
focused founder-led outreach to 200 hand-picked law firms in high-intake-volume practice areas,
supported by community partnerships that amplify trust.

## Part 1: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Stop marketing to "law firms." Start marketing to one specific type.

### Primary ICP

**Name:** The Overwhelmed Managing Partner  
**Firm size:** 1–8 attorneys  
**Practice areas (ranked by intake pain):**
1. Personal Injury (PI) — highest volume, most time-sensitive leads
2. Immigration — recurring document-heavy intake cycles
3. Family Law — emotionally driven, fast-response requirement
4. Criminal Defense — evening/weekend inquiries are critical
5. Estate Planning — seasonal volume spikes, form-heavy intake

**Geography:** Start with 2–3 metro areas where bar associations have active member engagement
(select based on your current pilots' locations — geographic clustering reduces travel/event cost).

**Psychographic:** Knows they're losing leads but doesn't know how to fix it without hiring.
Price-sensitive but ROI-oriented — will pay $150/month without blinking if you prove they're
recovering $3,000+/month in captured leads.

**Technology stack:** Likely uses Clio or MyCase for practice management. May use a basic contact
form on their website. Probably has no CRM. Definitely has Outlook or Gmail.

**Trigger events** (prioritize outreach to firms exhibiting these):
- Recent Google My Business reviews mentioning slow response
- LinkedIn posts from managing partner about "being overwhelmed"
- Job postings for a legal assistant or intake coordinator
- Recently launched new website
- Bar association membership renewal (active members are engaged buyers)

### Secondary ICP (Months 4–6 only)

Boutique practices that have recently hired a marketing coordinator or intake specialist. These
firms are already sold on the *need* — they need to be shown why software beats a person.

## Part 2: Positioning and Messaging

### Core Value Proposition

> "Stop losing clients while you sleep. [Product name] handles your intake 24/7 — capturing leads,
> collecting documents, and routing matters so you can focus on clients you've already won."

### Three Messaging Pillars

**1. The Loss Frame (lead with pain)**
- "33% of law firms never respond to inquiry calls. 72% of clients hire whoever responds first.
  If your intake isn't immediate, your competitors' is."
- Use this in: cold email subject lines, LinkedIn DMs, webinar intros.

**2. The ROI Frame (prove the number)**
- "Our customers typically recover 2–5 leads per month they were previously missing. At $3,000
  average matter value, that's $6,000–$15,000/month recovered from a $149/month tool."
- Use this in: demos, one-pagers, case studies, follow-up emails.

**3. The Trust Frame (remove the fear)**
- "Built for legal confidentiality. Your clients' data stays encrypted, compliant, and private.
  We're [SOC 2 Type I / working toward SOC 2 Type II] because your bar license requires it."
- Use this in: every first touchpoint, objection handling, website.

### Positioning vs. Competitors

| | Your product | Lawmatics | Clio | Gavel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary use case | Intake + doc collection | Full CRM + intake | Practice mgmt | Doc automation |
| Target firm size | 1–8 attorneys | 10–50+ attorneys | Any | Any |
| Pricing | $99–$149/mo | Custom (high) | $49–$169/user/mo | $83–$290/mo |
| Complexity | Simple, fast setup | High | High | Medium |
| Setup time | <1 day | Weeks | Weeks | Days |

**Your win zone:** The managing partner who wants intake fixed today, not a CRM implementation
project. Under 8 attorneys, under $200/month, live in under a day.

## Part 3: The 6-Month Execution Plan

### Month 1 — Convert Pilots and Build Foundation
**Customer target: 2–3 (pilot conversions)**

For each of your 3 pilots, schedule a 20-minute call this week. Ask:

1. "What's working? What's the most valuable thing you've gotten from using the product?"
2. "What's still a pain point?"
3. "If you had to put a number on it — how many leads do you think you've captured that you
   previously would have missed?"

Document exact outcomes. You need at least one number you can quote.

**Then send this offer within 48 hours of each call:**

it sounds like [product] is delivering real value for [Firm Name].

[Your name]

**Urgency note:** The 3-week deadline is real — hold it. If a pilot doesn't convert by the
deadline, have one follow-up call to understand why, then close the pilot and move on. A pilot
that won't convert after 30 days of use is not your customer.

**Build Target List (200 firms):**

Sources to use:

**LinkedIn Profile Optimization:**

Update your headline to: *"Helping small law firms capture every client with automated intake |

Write 3 LinkedIn posts before launching outreach:
- Post 1: "The $50,000 client most law firms never hear from" (intake statistics content)
- Post 2: Brief case study or outcome from a pilot (with permission)
- Post 3: "The 4-question intake audit for solo/small law firms" (useful tool/framework)

These posts establish context before cold DMs land. Recipients who check your profile see a
relevant, credible author — not a random sales pitch.

### Month 6 — Close Pipeline and Lock Retention
**Customer target: 1–3 new (cumulative: 15–22, goal = 20)**

#### Pipeline Close Sprint

Review every prospect in the pipeline at the start of month 6. For any deal that's been open
30+ days without a close:

1. Send a personal email: "I want to make sure we close this out one way or another."
2. Offer: 2 months at 50% off if they commit this month (equivalent to getting 1 month free).
3. If no response after this offer: remove from pipeline. Measure conversion rates.

#### Retention Lock

With 15–20 customers, churn becomes more important than acquisition.

Run a Net Promoter Score survey (single question: "On a scale of 1–10, how likely are you to
recommend [product] to a colleague?") with all customers.

For any customer who scores 6 or below:
- Call them directly within 24 hours.
- Understand the issue.
- Fix it if possible; if not, acknowledge it with a roadmap and offer a discount.

For any customer who scores 9–10:
- Ask for a case study quote and a referral.
- Nominate them for a "founding customer spotlight" (if you have any content channel).

**Churn target:** 0% through month 6. Every lost customer at this stage requires acquiring 2
new ones to stay on target. Retention is cheaper than acquisition.

## Part 6: Objection Handling Guide

### "We already have a system"

*"What does that system look like? Most firms I talk to have a web contact form or an email
address on the site — and when I ask what happens to a lead at 8pm, the answer is usually 'it
waits.' Is that true for you, or is yours genuinely automated?"*

Then listen. If they genuinely have automation: "That's great — what are you using?" (competitive
intel). If they're describing a manual process: "That's what I mean — that's the gap we close."

### "We're too small to need this"

*"The firms getting the most value from this are 1–3 attorney practices — exactly because
you don't have a receptionist or intake coordinator. The question isn't firm size, it's
how many inquiries you're getting. Are you seeing 3–5+ potential new client contacts per week?"*

### "What about client confidentiality?"

*"It's the first thing we built for. All data is encrypted at rest and in transit. We're
[SOC 2 compliant / on the SOC 2 roadmap]. No data is shared with third parties. We can share
our security documentation — what level of detail would your bar association require?"*

Never be defensive about this. Lead with it. Attorneys who ask are due-diligent buyers.

### "I can't afford it"

*"What's your average matter value? [Listen.] If we help you recover even one matter per month
that you were previously losing to slow intake, at [their matter value], that's [math]. The
tool pays for itself 5–10x over every month. Would it help to talk through what a realistic ROI
scenario looks like for your practice?"*

### "I need to think about it"

*"Of course — what's the main thing you want to think through? Is it the technology, the ROI,
or something about timing?"* (Always diagnose the real objection behind the stall.)

### "I'm not the only decision-maker"

This is rare at solo/small firms. If it comes up: *"Who else would be involved? I'm happy to
do a quick call with your [office manager / partner] to answer their questions directly."*

## Part 8: Decision Gates and Contingencies

### If pilot conversion rate is 0/3 (worst case)

This is a signal problem, not a product problem. Before any external outreach:
1. Interview each pilot: "What would it take for you to pay for this?"
2. If answer involves product gaps: fix the top-cited gap before outreach.
3. If answer involves trust/company stage: offer a 6-month free period in exchange for a
   public case study and reference calls — pay in social proof if not cash.
4. Do not launch cold outreach until you have at least 1 paying reference customer.

### If monthly customer targets are off-track by Month 3

Run a pipeline audit:
- If calls booked is low → message problem: rewrite email and DM sequences
- If calls to demos is low → qualification problem: loosen ICP or adjust qualification questions
- If demos to close is low → product/pricing problem: survey prospects who didn't close

Do not change budget allocation until the root cause is identified.

### If budget is running low before month 6

Preserve the reserve for LinkedIn paid ads only (months 5–6). Cut content writing before
cutting tools. Content is nice-to-have; the CRM and outreach tools are essential.

## Part 9: Pipeline Lag — Realistic Timing Expectations

**This section exists because the month-by-month targets look linear. They are not.**

### The Sales Cycle Reality

Even a 2–4 week close cycle has sequencing overhead:

- Week 1 of Month 1: Pilot calls + offer sent. Target list not yet built.
- Week 2–3 of Month 1: Target list built (200 firms). LinkedIn profile updated.
- Week 3–4 of Month 1: First 50 outreach touches sent (LinkedIn DMs + emails).
- Week 1–2 of Month 2: First replies, calls booked. Some discovery calls happen.
- Week 2–3 of Month 2: First demos. Some initial closes.
- Week 3–4 of Month 2: Close sprint from demos booked in weeks 1–2.

**Implication:** Month 2 realistically closes 1–2 customers from cold outreach (plus pilots
already converted). Month 3 is when the outbound engine hits full stride. The month-by-month
table in the summary is aspirational — the realistic distribution front-loads months 3–4:

| Month | Conservative Target | Aspirational Target |
|-------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 1 | 2 (pilots) | 3 (pilots) |
| 2 | 1–2 (early outbound) | 3–4 (if outbound ramped fast) |
| 3 | 3–4 (outbound in stride) | 4–5 |
| 4 | 4–5 (referrals + outbound) | 5–6 |
| 5 | 3–4 (trial + outbound) | 4–5 |
| 6 | 2–4 (close sprint) | 3–5 |
| **Total** | **15–21** | **22–28** |

Even the conservative path hits 20. But if month 2 closes fewer than expected: **do not panic**.
Increase pipeline building in months 2–3 to compensate. The lag is normal; the risk is stopping
outreach when early months underperform.

### Fast-Track Option for Months 1–2

Before cold outreach: mine warm intros. 

Ask each pilot customer: "Who else do you know at a firm like yours who might benefit from this?"
Ask every lawyer in your personal network: "Who should I be talking to?"

**Warm intros compress the sales cycle to 1–2 weeks vs. 3–4 weeks.** Even 3–5 warm intro
conversations in month 1 can produce 1–2 customers before cold outreach kicks in. This is
the highest-leverage use of the first two weeks.

**The 3 things that will make or break this plan:**

1. **Pilot conversion speed.** If pilots don't convert in month 1, you have no case study, no
   proof, and outreach becomes much harder. Make this the literal first thing you do.

2. **Message resonance.** Legal buyers respond to loss framing and ROI math, not feature lists.
   If outreach response rate is below 5%, the message is wrong — not the channel.

3. **Founder presence.** At this stage, you are the product. Every call, every email, every
   webinar builds trust that no tool can manufacture. Do not automate or delegate sales until
   you have 10 paying customers who can tell you what worked.
