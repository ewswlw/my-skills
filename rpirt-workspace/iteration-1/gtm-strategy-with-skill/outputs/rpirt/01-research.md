# 01 — Research

Phase 1 of RPIRT. All context for this phase drawn from user brief + external research.

---

## Goal

Launch a B2B SaaS product that automates client intake forms and document collection for small law
firms (1–10 attorney practices). Starting from zero paying customers, with 3 active pilots, $15k
in marketing budget, and a 6-month window to reach 20 paying customers. The deliverable is a
concrete, actionable GTM strategy that accounts for the limited budget, the specific buyer profile,
and the competitive dynamics of legal tech.

---

## Findings

### Market Context

**Legal tech buyer psychology:**
- Attorneys are conservative, risk-averse, and compliance-focused. Trust is the primary purchase
  criterion — security, data handling, peer references matter more than features.
- Small firm managing partners (1–10 attorneys) are sole decision-makers. No procurement committee,
  no IT department, no legal team to approve contracts. This shortens cycle vs. mid-market law.
- Despite being conservative, they're commercially motivated: 33% of law firms never respond to
  inquiries; 72% of consumers hire the first responding firm. Intake automation has a clear, loud
  ROI story that resonates immediately.

**Sales cycle benchmarks:**
- Large firm legal tech: 2–6 months typical cycle.
- Small firm (solo/2–10 attorney): significantly shorter — 2–6 weeks is achievable with a focused
  value prop and direct founder outreach. One decision-maker = faster close.
- Pilot to paid conversion: B2B opt-in pilot conversion averages 18–25%; structured pilots with
  clear success metrics can push 40–72% conversion.

**Market size and target density:**
- ~450,000 licensed attorneys in the US; ~80% work in firms of 10 or fewer.
- Solo/small firm segment represents the largest addressable pool but is diffuse (geographically
  spread, no central channel).
- Practice area matters: high-volume intake firms (PI, immigration, family, criminal defense,
  estate planning) have the most acute intake pain. Target these first.

### Competitive Landscape

**Practice management incumbents (not direct competitors but integrations needed):**
- Clio: $49–$169/user/month — market leader, 150k+ firms. Standard integration target.
- MyCase: $39–$119/user/month — strong small firm penetration.
- PracticePanther: $49–$99/user/month — solo/small focus.

**Direct competitors (intake + document automation):**
- Lawmatics: Full client acquisition CRM + intake automation. March 2026 launched AI Suite
  (QualifyAI, EngageAI, MerlinAI). Custom pricing, 3-user minimum. Positioned for growth-oriented
  mid-sized firms. Overkill for solo/small. Does not publish pricing (sales-heavy).
- Gavel: Document automation-first. $83–$290/month. Broader than intake only — contract drafting,
  automation templates.
- Relaw: $269–$349/month. Similar to Gavel.
- Rally: $99+/month/user. Similar.

**Positioning gap:** The competition skews toward either full-suite practice management (too broad,
too expensive, too complex for solo/small) or document automation (different primary use case).
A purpose-built, affordable intake + document collection tool for small firms (1–5 attorneys) has
real whitespace, particularly if priced at $99–$199/month and positioned around a single outcome:
"Never lose a lead to slow intake again."

### Budget Reality Check ($15,000 over 6 months)

- Legal PPC keywords cost $6–9/click. $15k in paid search = ~1,700–2,500 clicks → 15–20 leads
  at a 0.8% conversion → 0–1 customers. Paid ads are not viable at this budget.
- Cost per qualified outbound lead in B2B legal: $80–$150 via PPC. Via cold email/LinkedIn:
  $15–$40 if done with tight targeting.
- Effective $15k deployment: ~70% founder-led sales enablement (tools, lists, content assets),
  ~15% community/partnership activation, ~15% content/SEO seeding.

**Budget ceiling per customer:** $15,000 ÷ 20 = $750 max CAC. Easily achievable with founder-led
direct outreach. Impossible with paid media alone.

### Pilot-to-Paid Conversion Framework

- 3 active pilots must be the first priority — zero CAC customers.
- Structured conversion levers: define success metrics with each pilot, create a "graduation day"
  milestone, offer an incentive (charter pricing, first-year discount) for committing before pilots
  close.
- Pilot conversion rates: with structured approach, 60–70% achievable; even 2/3 converting = 2
  quick wins plus powerful case study content.
- Each converted pilot generates a case study asset worth ~5–10 new customer attributions via
  outreach and referrals.

### Pricing Benchmarks (from competitive research)

- Sweet spot for solo/small practice: $99–$199/month (approx. $1,200–$2,400 ARR).
- Annual billing discount (10–15%) reduces churn risk and improves cash flow.
- Consider tiered model: base (intake forms only) → pro (intake + doc collection + Clio/MyCase
  integration) → growth (multi-user + automation workflows).
- At $149/month average, 20 customers = $2,980 MRR / $35,760 ARR at 6 months.

### GTM Motion: Sales-Led vs. Product-Led

- PLG (product-led growth): Requires self-serve onboarding, low ACV, high volumes. Not viable at
  $15k budget with 0–20 customer range — discovery is too diffuse and product polish required for
  zero-touch is expensive.
- Sales-led: High-touch, founder-driven. Aligns with legal buyer's trust requirement. Correct
  motion for months 1–6.
- Hybrid in months 4–6: introduce a free 14-day trial for inbound after case studies built, while
  maintaining outbound founder sales.

### Early Customer Acquisition Playbook (0→20 SaaS)

From research across multiple sources, the proven path to first 20 customers:
1. Convert existing pilots first.
2. Mine founder's personal network and LinkedIn connections for warm intros.
3. Build one compelling case study / ROI proof asset before any outreach.
4. Run a focused "100 targets" outreach campaign — personalized, value-first, non-spammy.
5. Don't scale tactics until 10 customers — stay high-touch.
6. Add referral mechanics at 5+ customers.
7. Add community/partnership activation at months 3–4.

## Key Constraints

- **Budget:** $15,000 total, 6-month window. No room for paid media. Every dollar must be
  traceable to pipeline.
- **Target:** 20 paying customers by month 6 — requires approximately 3–4 new customers per month
  after converting pilots in month 1.
- **Starting position:** 3 pilot customers (free), 0 paying. Product has been validated with real
  users — this is an asset, not a liability.
- **Product specificity:** Intake forms + document collection — narrow enough to have a clear value
  prop, broad enough to serve multiple practice areas.
- **Team assumption:** Small founding team (1–3 people), founder-led sales required — no sales
  hire budget built into the $15k.
- **Sales cycle reality:** 2–6 weeks for small firms. 6-month runway to 20 customers means
  pipeline must be built by month 3 at the latest to close by month 6.
- **Non-negotiables:** Legal-grade data security messaging (SOC 2 roadmap or current cert needed
  in outreach), must address client confidentiality concerns in every touchpoint.
