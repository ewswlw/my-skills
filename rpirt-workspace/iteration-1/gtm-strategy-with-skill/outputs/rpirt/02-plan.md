# 02 — Plan

Phase 2 of RPIRT. Input: `rpirt/01-research.md` only.

---

## Durable Decisions

1. **Sales motion is sales-led, founder-driven for months 1–4.** PLG requires self-serve polish
   and volume that don't exist yet. High-touch is correct for legal buyers and the 0→20 range.
   Switch to hybrid (outbound + inbound trial) at month 5.

2. **Primary acquisition channel is founder LinkedIn + targeted cold email.** Paid media is
   economically infeasible at $15k total budget. Founder-led outreach is highest-ROI channel
   for this stage and buyer profile.

3. **Pilot conversion is the first priority.** 3 pilots represent zero-CAC revenue and case study
   material. No new outreach campaign launches until pilot conversion is actively managed.

4. **ICP is high-volume-intake small law firms: PI, immigration, family law, criminal defense,
   estate planning. 1–8 attorney practices.** These have the sharpest intake pain and clearest
   ROI story. Do not market to litigation boutiques, M&A firms, or transactional corporate shops
   in months 1–6.

5. **Pricing anchor: $149/month (monthly) or $129/month (annual).** Positions below Lawmatics
   (sales-heavy custom pricing), matches Gavel/Rally midpoint, keeps CAC math sound. Charter
   pricing for pilots and first-10 customers: $99/month locked for life.

6. **Security messaging is table-stakes, not differentiator.** Must address confidentiality and
   data handling in every outreach touchpoint. SOC 2 Type II is the goal; SOC 2 Type I or
   in-progress is acceptable positioning in months 1–4.

7. **No paid advertising in months 1–4.** If conversion rates validate the model after 10
   customers, allocate $2,500–$3,000 to LinkedIn Sponsored Content and Google in months 5–6.

8. **Case studies are the force multiplier.** Every closed customer in months 1–3 becomes
   a named case study (with permission). These are used in all subsequent outreach. Without
   social proof, outreach conversion is materially lower.

## Task Breakdown

### Phase A: Pilot Conversion (Month 1, Weeks 1–3)

**Task A1 — Pilot Audit and Success Metric Alignment**
- Meet with each of the 3 pilots individually.
- Document: What outcome have they experienced? What's still unresolved?
- Define 1–3 measurable success metrics with each pilot ("you've saved X hours on intake",
  "zero missed leads since go-live", etc.).
- Done when: Success scorecard exists for all 3 pilots with quantified outcomes.

**Task A2 — Pilot Conversion Offer**
- Create charter pricing offer: $99/month locked for life (vs. $149 list) for committing
  within 30 days of pilot graduation.
- Write a 1-page "Pilot Graduation Summary" per account: outcomes achieved, time saved,
  next steps to unlock full value.
- Done when: All 3 pilots have received a written conversion proposal with deadline.

**Task A3 — Case Study Creation (First Converter)**
- For first pilot who converts: produce a 400-word case study with specific metrics
  (time saved, leads captured, revenue recovered).
- Get a 2-sentence written quote for use in outreach.
- Done when: One published case study exists with a real firm name (or anonymized if required).

---

### Phase B: Foundation Assets (Month 1, Weeks 2–4)

**Task B1 — ICP Target List Build**
- Build a list of 200 targeted law firms in ICP practice areas (PI, immigration, family,
  criminal, estate planning). 1–8 attorneys. Use: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Martindale-Hubbell,
  Avvo, state bar directories.
- Segment by: practice area, firm size, geography (start with 2–3 metro areas).
- Done when: 200 verified firms with managing partner name + LinkedIn URL + email (if available).

**Task B2 — LinkedIn Profile Optimization**
- Rewrite founder LinkedIn headline and summary to speak to the ICP pain: "I help small law
  firms capture every client inquiry with automated intake."
- Post 3 pieces of content before launching outreach (not sales posts — useful content about
  intake conversion statistics, common intake mistakes, etc.).
- Done when: Profile updated; 3 posts published with >50 impressions each.

**Task B3 — Outreach Sequences Written**
- Write 3-step LinkedIn DM sequence (connection → value note → gentle ask).
- Write 3-email cold email sequence (pain-focused → case study → follow-up).
- Key constraint: Every sequence must address confidentiality and data security in message 1.
- Done when: Sequences peer-reviewed and loaded into outreach tool (Instantly.ai or Apollo).

**Task B4 — One-Page Leave-Behind (PDF)**
- Create a 1-page "ROI in 30 Days" PDF: intake problem stats, your product's solution, one
  case study metric, pricing, and a clear next step (14-day pilot).
- Done when: PDF designed, link-trackable (use Docsend or similar), ready to attach to outreach.

---

### Phase C: Outbound Engine (Months 2–3)

**Task C1 — LinkedIn Outreach Campaign**
- Send 20–30 personalized connection requests per week to target list (LinkedIn weekly limit
  is ~100; stay at 20–30 to avoid spam flags).
- Follow up with value message to each accepted connection.
- Track: requests sent → accepted → replies → calls booked.
- Budget: LinkedIn Sales Navigator ~$100/month ($200 for months 2–3).
- Done when: 200 contacts reached, ≥10 discovery calls booked from channel.

**Task C2 — Cold Email Campaign**
- Use Apollo.io or Hunter.io to verify emails for target list ($50–$100/month).
- Send 30 personalized emails per week with 3-step follow-up.
- Personalization: reference firm's practice area + a stat about intake loss in that area.
- Done when: 200 emails sent, ≥8 replies, ≥4 calls booked from channel.

**Task C3 — Discovery Call Execution**
- Run 30-minute calls: 20 min listening (what does intake look like today? what happens to
  inquiries at 6pm on Friday?), 10 min demo of relevant use case.
- Do not present pricing on call 1. Goal is to qualify fit and book demo.
- Done when: Call notes documented in CRM for every prospect; 8+ demos booked from calls.

**Task C4 — Demo + Close Process**
- 45-minute Zoom: 30-min demo tailored to practice area, 15-min objection handling.
- Standard objections to address: data security, integration with Clio/MyCase, setup time,
  "we already have a system."
- Offer: 14-day fully activated pilot → charter pricing if they commit at end.
- Done when: Close rate tracked (target: ≥30% of demos close to pilot or paid).

### Phase D: Community and Partnership Activation (Months 2–4)

**Task D1 — Bar Association Outreach**
- Identify 3–5 state/local bar associations in target metro areas.
- Propose: free 30-minute webinar on "Client Intake Best Practices for Small Firms."
- This is not a sales pitch — it's a value-driven session. Product mention is brief at the end.
- Budget: $0–$500 for collateral.
- Done when: ≥2 webinar commitments secured.

**Task D2 — Legal Tech Directories**
- List product on: Clio App Directory (if integrated), Capterra, GetApp, G2, Product Hunt.
- Write 3–5 detailed review solicitations to pilot customers.
- Done when: Listed on 4+ directories with ≥3 reviews.

**Task D3 — Referral Program Launch (at 5 paying customers)**
- Once 5 customers paying, launch a simple referral incentive: $100 credit per successful
  referral (one month free) for the referrer.
- Announce in onboarding email and directly to every paying customer.
- Done when: Referral program documented, email sent to all paying customers, ≥1 referral lead
  received by end of month 4.

**Task D4 — Legal Podcast and Newsletter Outreach**
- Identify 3–5 podcasts and newsletters targeting solo/small firm attorneys.
  (e.g., The Solo to Small Law Firm podcast, Attorney at Work, Above the Law Small Firms section)
- Pitch: Guest on intake/client acquisition topics. Not a product pitch.
- Budget: $0 (earned media).
- Done when: ≥1 podcast or newsletter placement secured by end of month 4.

### Phase E: Content and SEO Foundation (Months 2–5, background)

**Task E1 — Cornerstone Blog Posts (2 per month)**
- Topics: "Why 33% of Law Firms Lose Clients Before 9am", "The $50k Client Your Intake Form
  Is Losing", "How to Set Up a Legal Client Intake System in One Weekend."
- SEO targets: long-tail, low-competition ("legal client intake form template", "law firm
  intake automation small firm", etc.).
- Budget: $0 if founder writes; $200–$400/post if outsourced.
- Done when: 6 posts published with proper metadata by end of month 3.

**Task E2 — Free Resource (Lead Magnet)**
- Create one high-value free download: "The Small Law Firm Intake Audit Checklist" (PDF).
- Gate behind email capture on homepage.
- Done when: Checklist published, email capture working, ≥50 downloads by month 3.

---

### Phase F: Conversion Acceleration and Review (Months 4–6)

**Task F1 — Customer Success Touchpoints**
- Monthly check-in call with every paying customer (15 minutes).
- Track NPS or simple 1-question satisfaction score.
- Document quotes and outcomes for case studies.
- Done when: All customers have been contacted monthly; churn = 0 through month 6.

**Task F2 — Referral Push**
- At month 4, send personal email to every paying customer asking for 2–3 referrals.
- Offer: give their firm one month free for each referral who starts a paid subscription.
- Done when: ≥4 referral leads generated from existing base.

**Task F3 — Inbound Trial Funnel (Month 5+)**
- Add "Start Free 14-Day Trial" CTA to website homepage and blog.
- Build a 5-email onboarding sequence for trial users.
- Target: 10% trial-to-paid conversion from any inbound traffic.
- Done when: Trial flow live; ≥2 trial signups by end of month 5.

**Task F4 — Optional: LinkedIn Paid Amplification (Months 5–6)**
- If 10+ customers acquired and MRR justifies: allocate $2,500 to LinkedIn Sponsored Content
  targeting attorneys in ICP practice areas.
- Retarget website visitors. Boost best-performing case study post.
- Done when: Campaign launched, CPL tracked vs. organic baseline.

## Risk Mitigations

| Risk from Research | Mitigation |
|--------------------|------------|
| Pilot conversion rate is low (<2/3) | Structure pilots with explicit success metrics and graduation timeline before month 1 ends. Offer charter pricing urgency. Even 1/3 converts = case study + cashflow. |
| Sales cycle drags beyond 4 weeks | Use 14-day trial as the "yes" on-ramp — reduces commitment to try, not to buy. Track time-to-close weekly and eliminate process friction. |
| Founder LinkedIn network too small/wrong industry | Supplement with cold email + Apollo list from day 1. Don't depend on any single channel. |
| Competitor (Lawmatics AI Suite) creates confusion | Lean into "purpose-built for small firms, not enterprise CRM." Lawmatics has 3-user minimum and complex pricing. Position simplicity as the alternative. |
| Product not ready for self-serve trial (months 5–6) | Flag this as a dependency now. If onboarding isn't self-serve-ready by month 4, skip the trial funnel and continue pure sales-led. |
| Pilots refuse case study publication | Request permission early. Offer: use anonymized metrics only. Even an anonymous "immigration firm in Chicago" case study works. |
| Monthly target slip (e.g., 5 customers by month 3 instead of 10) | Identify slip by end of month 2, adjust ICP targeting or messaging — not budget. Root cause: wrong ICP, wrong message, or wrong channel. |
| No Clio/MyCase integration available | Address in outreach as "integration roadmap Q3." Build integration with Clio first — it has the largest install base. |
