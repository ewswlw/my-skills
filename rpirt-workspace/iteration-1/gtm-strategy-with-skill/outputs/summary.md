# RPIRT Run Summary
### Task: B2B SaaS GTM Strategy — Legal Intake Automation

---

## Complexity Assessment

**Classification: Complex (full 5-phase RPIRT)**

Rationale: The task had multiple strategic unknowns (channel efficacy, pilot conversion rates,
competitive landscape), a tight budget with a specific numeric target, and a real consequence
for getting the strategy wrong (spending $15k without hitting 20 customers). Single-pass
planning would have anchored on early assumptions without pressure-testing them. RPIRT's phase
separation caught a meaningful timeline flaw (month 2 over-optimism) and two CFO-level objections
that would have made the plan less credible in practice.

---

## Phase-by-Phase Summary

### Phase 1 — Research
**What happened:** External research across 6 web sources covering: legal tech GTM dynamics,
competitive pricing (Clio, MyCase, Gavel, Lawmatics, Rally, Relaw), CAC benchmarks for legal
tech, early-stage B2B SaaS 0→20 customer playbooks, buyer psychology at small law firms, and
cold email/pilot conversion best practices.

**Key findings that shaped the plan:**
- Legal PPC is $6–9/click — economically infeasible at $15k total. Forced a founder-led motion.
- Small firm managing partners are sole decision-makers — short cycle, high trust required.
- 33% of firms never respond to inquiries; 72% hire the first responder — built the core messaging.
- Lawmatics launched an AI Suite in March 2026 — positioned as complex/expensive vs. simple.
- Market depth is not a constraint: ~4,400+ ICP firms reachable in 2–3 metro areas.

**Artifact:** `rpirt/01-research.md`

---

### Phase 2 — Plan
**What happened:** Read only `01-research.md` as input. Produced a concrete execution plan
with 6 task phases (A–F), 15+ specific tasks with Definition of Done criteria, a line-item
budget, month-by-month customer targets, and risk mitigations for each research-flagged risk.

**Key planning decisions:**
- Sales-led motion (not PLG) for months 1–4 — forced by buyer psychology and budget.
- Pilot conversion first — zero-CAC customers before any outreach spend.
- Paid ads explicitly rejected until a 10-customer gate is met.
- $7.4k reserve flagged as "reserve" — later improved in Phase 4 fixes.

**Artifact:** `rpirt/02-plan.md`

---

### Phase 3 — Implement
**What happened:** Executed the plan to produce the full GTM deliverable (`gtm-plan.md`).
Added three improvements beyond the plan spec:
1. Week 1 immediate action protocol (founders need to start, not plan to start)
2. Full verbatim outreach templates — LinkedIn DM sequence + cold email sequence
3. Asset checklist as a launch gate

**Deviations from plan:** Lead magnet elevated from background task to first-class outreach
opener; paid ads reframed as a conditional decision gate (not just "optional").

**Artifact:** `rpirt/03-progress.md`, `gtm-plan.md` (Parts 1–8)

---

### Phase 4 — Review (recursive-refine protocol)
**What happened:** Generated a 10-criterion rubric specific to "B2B SaaS GTM strategy reviewed
by a budget-approving CFO." Scored the deliverable independently against each criterion.

**Round 1 result:**
- 9 criteria passed, 1 failed (Timeline Realism: 7/10 vs. 8/10 threshold)
- CFO adversarial review surfaced 3 additional issues: timeline optimism, vague budget reserve,
  founder bandwidth not accounted for

**Fixes applied:**
- Added Part 9: Pipeline Lag — realistic timing expectations with conservative vs. aspirational
  customer targets
- Added Part 10: Budget Reserve Deployment Framework — $7.4k broken into 4 named buckets with
  explicit deployment triggers
- Added Part 11: Founder Weekly Time Budget — hours per activity, what to cut when constrained

**Round 2 result:** All 10 criteria pass. Average score: 8.45/10.

**Artifact:** `rpirt/04-review.md`

---

### Phase 5 — Test
**What happened:** Validated the strategy across 6 test surfaces, simulating the founder's
execution experience:

1. **Financial math** — PASS. CAC ceiling holds at $750; MRR range $2,480–$2,980 at target.
2. **Timeline sequencing** — PASS. Week-by-week walkthrough found list-building timeline tight
   but workable; case study timing acceptable.
3. **Founder week 1 walkthrough** — PASS. Executable from day 1. One minor gap: CRM choice
   not pinpointed.
4. **Outreach message pressure test** — PASS. Messages calibrated for legal professional
   culture; loss framing and ROI math appropriate.
5. **Risk scenario: bad month 2** — PASS with caution. Plan survives 0 closes in month 2 if
   months 3–5 deliver 5+ each.
6. **Market availability** — PASS. 200-firm list is ~4.5% of reachable ICP in 2–3 metros.

**Artifact:** `rpirt/05-test-results.md`

## Final Deliverable

**File:** `outputs/gtm-plan.md`

The plan contains:
- ICP definition with practice area priority ranking and trigger events
- Positioning map vs. 6 named competitors
- Month-by-month execution plan (Months 1–6) with specific weekly activities
- Verbatim outreach templates (LinkedIn 3-step + cold email 3-step)
- Pilot conversion email template with charter pricing logic
- Line-item budget with deployment triggers
- Monthly OKRs + weekly tracking dashboard
- Objection handling guide (5 objections)
- Asset checklist (8 pre-launch gates)
- Decision gates and contingency protocols
- Pipeline lag reality check
- Founder time budget

## What RPIRT Added vs. Single-Pass Planning

A single-pass plan would have likely:
- Set month 2 targets at 3–4 customers without acknowledging pipeline lag
- Left $7.4k as an undifferentiated "reserve" with no deployment logic
- Not included founder time budgeting (the silent killer of GTM plans)
- Skipped the CFO stress test entirely

The Review phase caught all three of these, and the Test phase validated that the fixes held
under execution simulation. The final plan is meaningfully stronger than a first draft would have been.
