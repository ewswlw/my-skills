# Approach Summary: GTM Strategy Without RPIRT Skill
**Date:** 2026-04-12 | **Task:** B2B SaaS GTM for legal intake automation, 0→20 customers in 6 months

---

## What I Did

Produced a full 6-month go-to-market plan from scratch using direct reasoning, no specialized skill invocation.

### Process

1. **Grounded in constraints first.** I anchored everything to the three hard constraints — $15k budget, 3 pilots, 20 paying customers in 6 months — before choosing channels or tactics. This prevented the common mistake of recommending an expensive paid acquisition strategy that wouldn't fit the budget or timeline.

2. **Defined the ICP tightly.** Rather than "small law firms," I narrowed to 1–10 attorney firms in high-intake practice areas (personal injury, immigration, estate planning, family law). This specificity drives every channel and message choice downstream.

3. **Sequenced channels by trust and speed.** Small law firm buyers purchase on trust from people they know. I sequenced the plan: pilot conversion (highest trust, lowest effort) → referrals → community/webinars → outbound → paid. Paid channels were delayed to Month 4 deliberately, both to preserve budget and because messaging should be validated before you pay to amplify it.

4. **Built go/no-go gates.** Month 2 and Month 4 have explicit decision checkpoints so the founder isn't blindly executing a plan that isn't working. If cold email reply rates are below threshold by Month 2, the plan prescribes a diagnosis step rather than spending more.

5. **Made the math concrete.** Pricing recommendations, MRR targets, CAC targets, CPL thresholds, and conversion rate benchmarks are all specified. A GTM plan without numbers is a narrative, not a plan.

6. **Kept it founder-executable.** At this stage of the company, there is no sales team, no marketing team, and limited budget. Every recommendation is something a single non-marketer founder can actually execute, or can delegate cheaply.

---

## Key Strategic Choices Made (and Why)

| Choice | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Convert pilots before anything else | They are the lowest-cost path to revenue AND the source of early case studies/referrals |
| No paid ads until Month 4 | Budget is too small to waste on unvalidated messaging; test with free channels first |
| Webinar as primary Month 3 lever | Bar association webinars are a trusted, high-attendance channel for exactly the target buyer at near-zero cost |
| Rev-share partner channel | Zero upfront cost, leverages existing buyer relationships from Clio/MyCase consultants |
| Annual billing push | At $300 ACV, monthly churn can kill the business; annual billing is the single highest-leverage retention mechanism |

---

## What This Plan Does NOT Include

- Influencer marketing (wrong audience)
- Product Hunt launch (B2B niche, not a consumer app)
- Google Ads (high CPC in legal vertical, budget insufficient)
- Content at scale (SEO takes 3–6 months to pay off; included as foundation, not primary driver)

---

## Confidence & Assumptions

The plan is built on reasonable industry benchmarks (cold email 5–8% reply rates, demo-to-paid 20% overall conversion, webinar 10% conversion to trial). Actual results may vary significantly. The most important variable is the founder's ability to run a high-quality discovery call and demo — no amount of marketing spend compensates for a weak sales motion at this stage.

The 20-customer target is achievable but requires consistent execution every week, particularly in Months 1–3 when the pipeline is being built from scratch.
