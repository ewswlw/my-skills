# Go-to-Market Strategy: Legal Intake Automation SaaS
### First 6 Months Post-Launch
**Prepared:** 2026-04-12 | **Budget:** $15,000 | **Target:** 20 paying customers by Month 6

---

## Executive Summary

This GTM plan converts 3 pilot firms into paying anchors and adds 17 net-new paying customers over 6 months through a tightly focused outbound + community-led motion. With a $15k budget and no existing paying customers, the strategy prioritizes low-cost, high-trust channels — direct outreach, bar association communities, and referral loops — over paid acquisition. The plan is sequenced so each month builds on the last, with clear go/no-go checkpoints at Month 2 and Month 4.

**Core hypothesis:** Small law firm partners buy from people they trust. The fastest path to 20 customers is converting warm referrals and community credibility into pipeline, not broad advertising.

## 1. Market Sizing & Segment Focus

### Target Segment: "The Overwhelmed Solo-Small Firm"

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Firm size | 1–10 attorneys |
| Practice areas | Personal injury, estate planning, family law, immigration (highest intake volume) |
| Geography | Start with 2–3 metro areas (leverage pilot customer locations) |
| Pain profile | Spending 5–10 hrs/week on intake admin; losing leads to slow follow-up |
| Tech maturity | Uses Clio, MyCase, or Filevine; not averse to SaaS |
| Decision maker | Managing partner / owner-attorney (sole decision authority) |

### Why this segment first:
- Highest intake volume per attorney = strongest ROI story
- Solo/small partners make buying decisions in days, not quarters
- Underserved by enterprise tools (Lexicata/Clio Grow priced out or over-engineered)
- Word-of-mouth spreads fast within practice area communities

### Segments to avoid in Month 1–3:
- BigLaw (procurement cycles, IT review, lengthy security audits)
- Public defenders / government (budget constraints, procurement rules)
- Pure litigation firms with low intake volume

## 2. Positioning & Messaging

### One-Line Positioning
**"Turn your intake process from a leaky bucket into a closed loop — in under 30 minutes of setup."**

### Value Proposition Stack (prioritized by pilot feedback)

1. **Time savings:** Eliminate 5–8 hours/week of admin per intake coordinator
2. **Lead conversion:** Capture and follow up with prospective clients before they call your competitor
3. **Document completeness:** Stop chasing clients for missing forms; automated reminders do it for you
4. **Client experience:** Professional, mobile-friendly intake that signals competence before the first meeting

### Messaging by Persona

**Managing Partner (economic buyer):**
> "Every lost intake is a lost case. Our platform ensures no lead falls through the cracks and every new client arrives with their documents complete — so you spend time lawyering, not chasing paperwork."

**Office Manager / Paralegal (champion/user):**
> "Set it up once. It sends reminders, collects documents, and notifies you when everything is ready. No more phone tag, no more incomplete files."

### Differentiation from Competitors

| Competitor | Their gap | Our edge |
|---|---|---|
| Clio Grow | Expensive, complex, full CRM | Intake-only focus, 10x faster setup |
| Lawmatics | Marketing-heavy, agency-oriented | Built for the firm, not the agency |
| Generic form tools (Typeform, JotForm) | No legal document logic | Practice-area templates, e-sign, doc collection |
| Paper/email intake | Manual, lossy | Full automation, audit trail |

### Proof Points to Develop (Month 1 Priority)
- Case study: Pilot firm A — hours saved per week, leads captured
- Testimonial: Direct quote from managing partner
- Before/after: Old intake flow vs. new flow (visual)

## 4. Month-by-Month Execution Plan

### Month 1: Convert Pilots & Build the Machine (Weeks 1–4)

**Goal:** 3 paying customers (convert all 3 pilots), pipeline of 20+ qualified leads

**Pilot Conversion (Week 1–2)**
- Personal call with each pilot firm: structured discovery on what's working and what isn't
- Present formal pricing with early-adopter discount (20–30% off Year 1)
- Offer 90-day money-back guarantee to de-risk the decision
- Ask for 1 referral as part of onboarding ("Who else in your network has this problem?")
- Target close all 3 by end of Week 2
- Budget: $1,200 (discounts / small onboarding gifts for pilots)

**Messaging Validation (Week 1–3)**
- Draft 3 cold email sequences with different value prop angles (time savings vs. lead conversion vs. document completeness)
- Build a 50-contact prospect list using Apollo.io (personal injury + estate planning + immigration, 1–10 attorney firms, metro areas near pilots)
- Send 15 emails per variant; track open rates, reply rates, meeting book rates
- Winning variant becomes the primary sequence
- Budget: $400 (Apollo.io)

**Foundation Assets (Week 2–4)**
- Write 2 case studies from pilot firms (get sign-off on names or anonymize)
- Record 3-minute product demo video
- Build a simple comparison landing page (vs. paper intake, vs. Clio Grow)
- Set up HubSpot free CRM with pipeline stages: Prospect → Contacted → Demo Booked → Proposal → Closed
- Budget: $600 (design for case studies + landing page polish)

**Month 1 Success Metrics:**
- Pilots converted to paying: 3/3
- Cold email reply rate: ≥ 8%
- Demo meetings booked from cold outreach: ≥ 3
- Pipeline (qualified leads): ≥ 20

### Month 2: Outbound at Scale + First Community Plays (Weeks 5–8)

**Goal:** 5 total paying customers (+2 new), 40+ qualified leads in pipeline

**Scaled Cold Outbound**
- Ramp to 50–75 personalized cold emails/week using winning Month 1 sequence
- Add LinkedIn connection requests (20/day) with a soft intro message — no pitch in first message
- Build a 200-contact outbound list targeting the 4 highest-volume practice areas
- Hire a part-time VA ($300–400/month) to manage list building and initial research if founder bandwidth is constrained
- Budget: $800 (Apollo data, VA for 2 weeks)

**Bar Association Entry**
- Identify 3–5 local/state bar associations with active solo/small firm sections
- Join as a vendor member or sponsor a section newsletter ($200–400 each)
- Offer to present a free 20-minute webinar: *"5 Ways Intake Automation Pays for Itself in 30 Days"*
- First webinar scheduled for end of Month 2 or early Month 3
- Budget: $600 (2 bar association memberships/sponsorships)

**Referral Program Launch**
- Email all 5 paying customers: introduce the referral program
- Offer: $200 account credit (or 1 free month) for each paying referral
- Create a simple referral landing page with a unique tracking link per customer
- Budget: $400 (setup + first payouts)

**Month 2 Success Metrics:**
- Paying customers: 5
- Cold email meetings booked (cumulative): ≥ 8
- Bar association contacts made: ≥ 1 confirmed speaking/webinar slot
- Pipeline: ≥ 40 qualified leads

**Month 2 Go/No-Go Gate:**
- If cold outbound reply rate < 5% AND no demos converting → pause outbound, rewrite messaging, do 10 customer discovery calls before proceeding
- If 0 pilots converted to paying → pivot pricing model or offer extended trial before paid acquisition

### Month 3: Warm Channels + Content Foundation (Weeks 9–12)

**Goal:** 9 total paying customers (+4 new)

**Webinar & Community Conversion**
- Host the bar association webinar (target 30–50 attendees from small firms)
- Structure: 15 min education (intake ROI), 5 min live demo, 5 min Q&A
- Offer webinar-only promo: 60-day free trial + 20% off Month 1 for attendees who sign up within 7 days

**Legal Tech Community Presence**

**SEO Content (Start Now for Month 5–6 Payoff)**
- Publish 3 long-form articles targeting low-competition, high-intent keywords:
  1. "How to automate client intake for small law firms" (informational)
  2. "Best client intake software for solo attorneys 2026" (comparison)
  3. "How much time does client intake take? [Law firm benchmark]" (data-driven)
- Distribute to JD Supra, Attorney at Work, and the bar association newsletter
- Budget: $1,200 (freelance legal content writer for 3 articles, or founder-written with editing)

**Month 3 Success Metrics:**

### Month 4: Systematize What's Working (Weeks 13–16)

**Goal:** 13 total paying customers (+4 new)

**Double Down on Winning Channel**
- By now, data will show which channel (outbound, webinar, referral, community) has the best CAC
- Allocate 60% of remaining budget and founder time to the top channel
- If outbound is winning: scale to 100 emails/week, add LinkedIn voice notes for top prospects
- If webinar is winning: schedule second webinar and pitch to 2 more bar associations
- If referral is winning: increase referral incentive to $300 and actively ask top customers for intros

**LinkedIn Paid Ads — Test Budget**
- Audience: Job title = "Managing Partner" OR "Attorney" + Company size 1–10 + Industry = "Law Practice"
- Ad type: Lead gen form (download a free "Law Firm Intake Audit" template)
- Budget: $1,000 for Month 4 test
- Target: ≥ 30 leads at ≤ $33 CPL; ≥ 2 demos booked
- If CPL > $50 → pause, rework creative. If CPL < $33 → scale in Month 5–6.

**Partner Channel Initiation**
- Identify 5 legal technology consultants / Clio implementation partners who serve small firms
- Offer: 20% revenue share for any paying customer they refer
- These partners have existing trust with your exact buyer; even 1–2 active partners can be significant
- Budget: $0 upfront (rev-share only)

**Month 4 Success Metrics:**
- Paying customers: 13
- Identified top-performing channel: Yes (data-driven decision documented)
- LinkedIn CPL: ≤ $33
- Partner conversations initiated: ≥ 5

## 5. Pricing Strategy

### Recommended Pricing Model

| Tier | Price | Target Firm Size | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | $149/mo | Solo attorney | 1 user, 50 intakes/mo, 5 templates |
| **Growth** | $299/mo | 2–5 attorneys | 3 users, unlimited intakes, 20 templates, e-sign |
| **Studio** | $499/mo | 6–10 attorneys | 10 users, unlimited everything, white-label, priority support |

**Pilot-to-Paid Transition:**
- Offer pilots the Growth plan at Starter pricing for Year 1 (effective 50% discount)
- Frame as "founding customer" rate, locked in as long as they remain a customer
- This creates urgency to convert and loyalty to retain

**Annual vs. Monthly:**
- Push annual billing: offer 2 months free (effectively ~17% discount)
- Annual billing dramatically improves cash flow and reduces churn risk
- Target: ≥ 60% of new customers on annual by Month 6

**MRR Target at 20 Customers (blended Growth plan estimate):**
- 20 customers × ~$280 average blended MRR = ~$5,600 MRR ($67k ARR)

## 6. Sales Process

### Sales Motion: Founder-Led, High-Touch

At sub-$500/month ACV with a trust-sensitive buyer, a founder-led sales process is the only viable approach in Month 1–4. Do not hire sales until Month 5–6 at the earliest, and only if pipeline justifies it.

### The 5-Step Close

1. **Discovery call (20 min):** Understand current intake process, volume, pain. Ask: "What happens when a potential client fills out your contact form right now?" Let them describe the mess.
2. **Personalized demo (30 min):** Demo a practice area template matching their firm. Show the client-facing flow on mobile. Show the document collection screen. Show the intake dashboard.
3. **ROI calculation (live in the call):** "If you save 6 hours/week at your billing rate of $X, that's $Y/month. Our Growth plan is $299." Let the math close for you.
4. **Proposal email (within 2 hours of demo):** Single-page PDF with pricing, what's included, and a link to start a 14-day trial. Include a testimonial quote from a pilot firm.
5. **Follow-up (Days 3, 7, 14):** Value-add, not pestering. Day 3: send a relevant article. Day 7: offer to set up their first template live on a 15-minute call. Day 14: last check-in, then move to nurture.

### Sales Velocity Target

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Cold outreach to demo booked | ≤ 5% (i.e., 100 emails = 5 demos) |
| Demo to trial | ≥ 40% |
| Trial to paid | ≥ 50% |
| Overall cold-to-paid | ≥ 1% |
| Average sales cycle | 14–21 days |

## 8. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pilot firms don't convert | Medium | High | Lock in conversion before Month 1 ends; diagnose with discovery call |
| Cold email deliverability issues | Medium | Medium | Warm domains properly (2 weeks); keep volume ≤ 50/day/domain |
| Long sales cycles delay Month 6 target | Medium | High | Front-load outreach; build pipeline 2x your close target |
| Competitor launches similar product at lower price | Low-Medium | High | Double down on customer success and switching costs; differentiate on service |
| Churn from early customers (product gaps) | Medium | High | Weekly check-ins with all customers through Month 3; fast bug/feature response |
| LinkedIn CPL too high to be viable | Medium | Medium | Gate LinkedIn behind Month 4; don't bet on it early |
| Founder bandwidth bottleneck | High | High | Prioritize ruthlessly; Month 1–2 is outbound only; don't start content + ads + events simultaneously |
